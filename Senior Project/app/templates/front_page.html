Steganography Project

Introduction

For my senior project, I decided to explore the fascinating subject of steganography. Steganography is a technique used to hide information in plain sight. This was commonly used during World War II for allies to communicate secret messages. During this time, steganography was performed through various techniques, including: invisible ink, knitting, and ciphers within written messages. With the rapid evolution of technology, more sophisticated methods of steganography have developed. With modern technology, we can now hide information within the pixels of a digital image, the frames of video and audio files, and even within network packets!

In this project, I have implemented steganography using the Least Significant Bit technique. This method of steganography hides information within the rightmost bit in each binary number of a file. Here, we will be converting a text message into binary and hiding it within the least significant bits of image and audio files. The new, modified image and audio files holding the hidden message will look and sound the same as the original files.
